Project Management Approach

PM Methodology & Framework

  • Waterfall methodology selected due to regulatory requirements, infrastructure dependencies, and banking compliance needs requiring sequential phase approvals
  • Aligned with PMI/PMBOK standards across all five process groups: Initiation (charter, stakeholder identification), Planning (comprehensive project plans), Executing (coordinated construction), Monitoring & Controlling (EVM, variance analysis), Closing (lessons learned, handover)
  • Structured approach essential for achieving Uptime Institute Tier 4 certification requiring rigorous documentation and phase validation

Procurement & Contract Management

  • Developed comprehensive RFP/RFQ documentation defining technical specifications (Tier 3/4 requirements), commercial terms, qualification criteria, evaluation methodology
  • Evaluated 8-12 contractor bids using weighted scoring matrix: Technical capability (60%), Commercial proposal (25%), Experience & references (15%)
  • Negotiated fixed-price contracts with milestone-based payments (10-15 milestones per project), 10% retention held until defect liability period completion, performance bonds (10% contract value)
  • Managed contract variations through formal change control: 15-25 change orders per project, each with detailed cost/schedule impact analysis and client approval
  • Vendor compliance verification: Insurance certificates (professional indemnity, public liability), DEWA contractor licenses, safety certifications, quality management system certifications (ISO 9001 where applicable)

Earned Value Management & Performance Measurement

  • Implemented Earned Value Management for all projects >$1.5M tracking project performance against baseline
  • Cost Performance Index (CPI): 1.02-1.05 across all projects indicating under-budget performance (every $1.00 spent delivered $1.02-$1.05 of value)
  • Schedule Performance Index (SPI): 0.98-1.02 indicating on-schedule or minor acceptable variance
  • Estimate at Completion (EAC): Monthly forecast showed final cost within 3-5% of baseline budget across all 7 facilities
  • Tracked Planned Value (PV), Earned Value (EV), Actual Cost (AC) weekly providing early warning of budget/schedule risks

Stakeholder Engagement & Communication Management

  • Conducted comprehensive stakeholder analysis identifying 30-40 stakeholders per project across client executives (CIO, CFO, VP Operations), department heads (IT, Facilities, Security), consultants (MEP, data center specialist, structural), contractors (8-12 specialized), vendors (Dell, HP, Cisco, EMC, Eaton, Trane), authorities (DEWA, Civil Defense, Central Bank inspectors)
  • Power/Interest Matrix categorization: Key Players (client executives, Central Bank inspectors - high power/high interest), Keep Satisfied (DEWA, consultants - high power/lower interest), Keep Informed (department heads - lower power/high interest), Monitor (sub-contractors - lower power/interest)
  • Developed Communication Management Plan specifying stakeholder-specific protocols: Steering Committee (monthly executive presentations), Project Board (weekly tactical meetings), Contractor coordination (daily toolbox meetings), Client departments (bi-weekly progress updates), Authorities (as-needed for approvals/inspections)
  • Email communication volume: 25-50 emails/day during active phases coordinating across all stakeholder groups

Quality Management

  • Developed comprehensive Quality Management Plan defining quality standards (Uptime Institute Tier 3/4, TIA-942, manufacturer specifications), acceptance criteria, inspection schedules, roles/responsibilities
  • Quality Assurance (QA): Process audits ensuring contractor adherence to approved procedures, document reviews verifying completeness, compliance checks against specifications
  • Quality Control (QC): Physical inspections at quality hold points (raised floor grid, electrical rough-in, cable termination, equipment installation), testing and commissioning, as-built verification
  • Established quality hold points: 15-20 per project preventing work progression until inspection and approval
  • Tracked quality metrics: Defect density (defects per 100 inspections), rework percentage (target <5%), first-time pass rate (target >90%), non-conformance trends

Project Governance Structure

  • Steering Committee: Monthly executive oversight with client CIO, CFO, VP-level, PM presenting. Decision authority for budget changes >5%, scope changes, major risk escalations
  • Project Board: Weekly tactical decision-making with client project manager, consultant lead, main contractor manager, PM chairing. Authority for operational decisions, minor scope clarifications, contractor coordination issues
  • Change Control Board: Bi-weekly evaluation of change requests. Assessed changes for technical impact, cost, schedule, risk
  • Decision Authority Matrix: PM authority (operational decisions, <$10K changes), Project Board (tactical decisions, $10K-$50K changes), Steering Committee (strategic decisions, >$50K changes, major risks)
  • Stage-Gate Reviews: Formal approvals required at major milestones (design completion, procurement completion, construction 50%, pre-commissioning, final acceptance)
